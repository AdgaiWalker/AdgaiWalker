# Dianzi Opening Animation Design

Date: 2026-06-25

## Core Intent

This opening animation is not a logo reveal. It is a short symbolic event:

> A valuable idea is pressed into reality by a person. It breaks the old picture, reorganizes possibility, and remains unfinished so people can keep making it real.

"Dianzi" is a seed of a solution. It comes from lived experience, real problems, accidental inspiration, and human effort. It is not the problem itself. It is a possible way to make something valuable exist in the world.

AI should not appear as a visible character, robot, chip, or data gimmick. It should be felt as an invisible organizing force behind the product: a way to help human ideas move toward reality.

## Emotional Target

The user's reaction should be:

> Wow, it can be done like this.

The surprise should come from a shift in perception. At first, the user thinks they are watching a brush touch paper. Then they realize the paper is a thin membrane of reality, the dot is not ink, and the action opens a process where an idea begins to become real.

## Symbol Map

- Paper: the membrane between thought and reality.
- Hand: human will, attention, and effort.
- Brush: a precise tool for pressing an idea into the world.
- Black dot: the first planted point of a solution.
- Collapse: reality's pressure gathering around the idea.
- Fragmentation: the old picture breaking apart so a new structure can form.
- Flying fragments: life, problems, constraints, tools, memories, interfaces, and future possibilities.
- Rotation: a change of perspective.
- Purple arc: the path from origin to realization.
- Small purple dot: a future landing point that has not fully arrived.
- Unfinished logo state: the idea has structure and direction, but still needs people to continue it.

## Visual Principle

The scene should feel cool, precise, independent, harmonious, and lightly technological without becoming an AI-cliche visual.

Avoid:

- robots, chips, hologram hands, neural network cliches
- blue sci-fi dashboards
- generic explosive particles
- traditional ink-spreading effects
- a fully closed, over-finished logo ending

Prefer:

- warm white paper
- controlled shadows behind thin paper
- precise spatial motion
- black and pale purple structure
- fragments that feel like life and product material, not decoration
- a final state that breathes quietly and stays unfinished

## Storyboard

### 1. Paper-Back View

The camera begins on the far side of a single sheet of warm white paper. The user is visually behind the paper, not under a table. The paper fills the screen and feels thin, tactile, and slightly translucent.

Behind the paper, a hand and brush are visible only as softened shadow forms. The world beyond the paper is not a full scene. It is an implied living world: slight light movement, vague traces of notes, images, messages, paths, or daily moments. These traces must remain subtle.

Purpose: establish that ideas come from life, not from a blank abstract void.

### 2. Descent

The hand guides the brush downward with slow control. The brush tip becomes sharper and darker as it approaches the paper surface. The user feels that something is about to be pressed through the membrane.

The movement should be calm and intentional. No writing stroke happens. The brush only descends.

Purpose: make the act human and deliberate.

### 3. Implant

The brush tip touches the paper.

For a short instant, time holds. The paper indents. A pure black dot appears at the contact point as if pressed into the paper from the other side.

The dot is not ink spreading. It is a dense point planted into reality.

Purpose: mark the moment a solution-seed enters the world.

### 4. Spatial Collapse

After the dot appears, space begins to collapse toward it. The paper surface subtly bends inward. Nearby visual traces drift toward the point. Light narrows toward the center.

This collapse should feel like pressure, not destruction. It represents experience, problems, constraints, resources, and desire being compressed around one possible solution.

Purpose: show that valuable ideas must carry the weight of reality.

### 5. Fragment Break

The paper image breaks into fragments. The break should feel surprising and cool, but not violent or chaotic for its own sake.

Fragments can include:

- blurred text remnants
- unfinished interface lines
- photo-edge pieces
- route or path marks
- voice waveform strips
- small note-like blocks
- blank future frames

The fragments burst outward for a brief moment, then begin to curve around the dot.

Purpose: the old picture breaks so possibility can be reorganized.

### 6. Perspective Flip

The camera rotates with the fragments, moving from the paper-back view into a cleaner frontal paper view. This is the main perceptual surprise.

The flip should feel like the user has passed through the dot, not like a normal scene cut. The user should feel: "I was not just watching paper. I was watching an idea cross into reality."

Purpose: express that a real idea changes the angle from which the world can be seen.

### 7. Reorganization

As the perspective settles, the fragments are pulled into a quiet structure. They do not assemble into a fully completed object. Instead, they leave behind a minimal system:

- black dot
- pale purple arc
- small purple dot
- faint path or blueprint traces
- partially revealed Dianzi identity

The fragments should disappear into the structure as if they have become direction, not debris.

Purpose: the idea is no longer just a thought; it now has form, path, and next step.

### 8. Unfinished Final State

The final state should not feel like a closed brand bumper.

The black dot rests slightly left of center or in the visual position required by the brand mark. The pale purple arc remains incomplete. The small purple dot appears near the arc but retains a tiny sense of suspended possibility.

"Dianzi" can softly reveal, but it should not overpower the unfinished structure. "DIANZI" and the supporting line "用点子连接人与 AI" may appear lightly, delayed, or at reduced emphasis.

The final screen should feel stable but alive. A very subtle breath or residual charge can remain in the small purple dot or arc.

Purpose: the idea has begun to become real, but it still needs people to continue it.

## Motion Timing

Suggested total duration: 6 to 8 seconds.

- Paper-back stillness: 0.8s
- Brush descent: 1.2s
- Touch and hold: 0.25s
- Dot implant: 0.25s
- Collapse: 0.9s
- Fragment burst: 0.45s
- Fragment orbit and perspective flip: 1.1s
- Reorganization: 1.0s
- Unfinished final reveal: 1.2s

The strongest "wow" moment should be the fragment break plus perspective flip, not the final logo state.

## Product Meaning

The animation should communicate this product belief:

> Ideas become valuable when people use them to make something real.

Dianzi is not merely a place to record ideas. It should feel like a product that helps useful and valuable ideas move toward reality in ways that improve human life.

## Final Creative Line

> 点下去的不是墨，是一个未来的现实。

Alternative internal line:

> 一个有价值的点子，被人点入现实；它打碎旧画面，重组可能性，然后等待被继续完成。

## Open Decision For Later

The implementation still needs one visual choice before production:

- Conservative unfinished ending: core logo structure plus soft partial text.
- Bold unfinished ending: only the core mark appears first; text reveals later through pause, scroll, or interaction.

Recommended direction: conservative unfinished ending. It preserves brand recognition while keeping the sense that the idea is still becoming real.
